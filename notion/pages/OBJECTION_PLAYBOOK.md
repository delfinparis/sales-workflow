## The Golden Rules

1. **Never argue** - Acknowledge their concern first
2. **Ask questions** - Understand the real objection
3. **Use stories** - "I had another agent who felt the same way..."
4. **Create space** - "No pressure" is your friend

---

## Common Objections & Responses

### "I need to think about it"

**What they might mean:**
- Not enough info
- Need to talk to spouse/partner
- Comparing to competitors
- Polite rejection

**Response:**
> "Totally understand - this is a big decision! What specifically would you want to think through? Maybe I can help clarify."

**Follow-up questions:**
- "Is there anything I didn't cover that would be helpful?"
- "Would it help to talk to some of our current agents?"
- "Is there someone else you need to loop in on this decision?"

---

### "I'm talking to other brokerages"

**What they might mean:**
- Doing due diligence
- Have a preferred choice
- Leveraging for better offer

**Response:**
> "Smart to explore your options! What's drawing you to them? I want to make sure you're comparing apples to apples."

**Follow-up questions:**
- "What do you like most about what they're offering?"
- "What would make you choose one over the other?"
- "Would a comparison chart be helpful?"

**Pro tip:** Don't trash competitors. Focus on Kale's unique value.

---

### "The timing isn't right"

**What they might mean:**
- Busy with current deals
- Personal life issues
- Waiting for something specific
- Not actually interested

**Response:**
> "I hear you - timing matters. When would be better? What would need to change?"

**Follow-up questions:**
- "What's making now not the right time?"
- "Is there a specific milestone you're waiting for?"
- "Would it help if I checked back in [timeframe]?"

**Action:** Set specific follow-up date, don't leave open-ended

---

### "I'm happy where I am"

**What they might mean:**
- Genuinely content
- Don't know what they're missing
- Loyal to current broker
- Avoiding change

**Response:**
> "That's great! What's working well for you there? I'm always curious what keeps top agents happy."

**Follow-up questions:**
- "If you could change one thing, what would it be?"
- "How long have you been there?"
- "What would make you consider a change?"

**Pro tip:** Plant seeds for later. Stay in touch without pushing.

---

### "Your splits aren't good enough"

**What they might mean:**
- Money is top priority
- Don't see other value
- Comparing raw numbers only

**Response:**
> "I get it - the numbers matter. Can I show you the full picture? When you factor in [fees, support, leads, etc.], here's what it actually looks like..."

**Follow-up questions:**
- "What are your total fees right now?"
- "Are you factoring in desk fees, E&O, transaction fees?"
- "What would you need to see to make it work?"

**Pro tip:** Focus on net, not gross. Show total cost of doing business.

---

### "I'm worried about leaving my clients"

**What they might mean:**
- Fear of transition logistics
- Client relationships at risk
- Non-compete concerns

**Response:**
> "Your clients are YOUR clients - that doesn't change. Here's exactly how the transition works..."

**Explain:**
- Clients follow the agent, not the brokerage
- Transition timeline and process
- How we help with communication
- Success stories of smooth transitions

---

### "What if it doesn't work out?"

**What they might mean:**
- Fear of regret
- Bad past experience
- Need reassurance

**Response:**
> "Fair question. Here's our track record... [share retention rate, success stories]. And honestly, if it's not a fit, you'd know pretty quickly."

**Share:**
- Agent retention numbers
- Specific success stories
- What support looks like in first 90 days
- "No long-term contracts" (if applicable)

---

### "I don't know anyone there"

**What they might mean:**
- Value relationships
- Worried about culture fit
- Need social proof

**Response:**
> "Totally get that - culture matters. Would you like to talk to a few of our agents? I can connect you with someone in a similar situation."

**Offer:**
- Agent references (match by experience level, niche)
- Team event invite
- Office visit
- Coffee with current agents

---

### "Let me talk to my spouse/partner"

**What they might mean:**
- Legitimate need to discuss
- Delay tactic
- Partner has concerns

**Response:**
> "Of course! Would it help if I joined a call with both of you? Happy to answer any questions they might have."

**Follow-up questions:**
- "What do you think their main concern will be?"
- "Is there specific info I can prepare for that conversation?"
- "When do you think you'll have a chance to talk?"

---

## Objection Prevention

**Things to cover BEFORE objections come up:**

| Topic | Address Proactively |
|-------|---------------------|
| Splits | "Let me walk you through our full compensation structure..." |
| Support | "Here's what your first 90 days would look like..." |
| Culture | "Let me tell you about a few agents on our team..." |
| Transition | "Moving brokerages is easier than you might think..." |
| Timing | "There's never a perfect time, but here's why now works..." |
